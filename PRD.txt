# PDR — Social Signal Investment Assistant (v1)

## 1. Product Summary

A system that monitors a curated list of X accounts, interprets their posts, clusters related posts into higher-level events, and translates them into explainable, multi-day investment suggestions for a **small, focused tradable universe**.

The initial universe will be intentionally narrow and centered on **tech and AI-related assets**, with the ability to expand later.

The system is **not** an autonomous trading system in v1.
It is a **decision-support engine** that produces explainable recommendations:

* `BUY`
* `HOLD`
* `SELL`

It will also explicitly explain:

* why an action was proposed
* why no action was proposed
* why a signal was vetoed or downgraded

---

## 2. Problem

Monitoring a handful of high-signal X accounts manually is slow, inconsistent, and prone to bias. Useful information gets mixed with noise, hype, repeated narratives, and opinion masquerading as fact.

The product should help answer:

> “Given the accounts I trust enough to monitor, and a narrow asset universe I care about, what should I do over a multi-day horizon?”

The system must do this in a way that is:

* constrained
* auditable
* explainable
* expandable later

---

## 3. Product Goals

### Primary goals

* Convert monitored X posts into structured investment suggestions.
* Focus on **multi-day reactions**, not intraday trading.
* Restrict the decision space to a narrow action set and a focused asset universe.
* Provide strong explainability for both positive and negative decisions.
* Merge repeated aligned posts across monitored accounts into a single higher-level signal/event.

### Secondary goals

* Build a reusable architecture that can later support:

  * broader asset coverage
  * simulation/backtesting
  * decision logging with locked prices
  * paper trading or execution workflows

---

## 4. Non-goals for v1

* No autonomous execution
* No intraday trading
* No short-selling logic
* No unrestricted asset discovery
* No open-ended “trade anything” behavior
* No portfolio optimization engine
* No real simulation engine yet

---

## 5. Initial Product Scope

## 5.1 Tradable Universe

The initial asset universe will be:

* relatively small
* manually curated
* focused on **tech and AI**

Examples of likely starting buckets:

* AI/semiconductor stocks
* large-cap tech stocks
* selected AI/tech ETFs
* selected crypto assets with strong relevance to monitored narratives

Exact symbols will be defined in a later iteration.

The system must support future expansion into:

* more equities
* ETFs
* crypto
* bonds and other instruments that can be bought/sold in shares or accessible units

---

## 5.2 Action Space

For v1, recommended action set:

* `BUY`
* `HOLD`
* `SELL`

### Why this is the right choice

This is simpler and more honest than adding:

* short
* reduce/add sizing categories
* hedges
* derivative-specific actions

You said multi-day, no daily trading. That points to **discrete directional suggestions**, not hyper-granular execution logic.

### Interpretation

* `BUY`: signal suggests initiating or increasing long exposure
* `HOLD`: signal suggests maintaining position or taking no change
* `SELL`: signal suggests exiting or reducing long exposure

---

## 5.3 Time Horizon

Target holding / reaction horizon:

* **multi-day**
* acceptable end-to-end decision latency: **up to 12 hours**

That means the architecture should optimize for:

* quality over speed
* event grouping over instant reaction
* explainability over ultra-low-latency inference

---

## 6. Key Design Principles

1. **Narrow before broad**
   Small asset universe, small action space, few monitored accounts.

2. **Controlled, not fully agentic**
   The system should use bounded LLM modules, not an unconstrained autonomous agent.

3. **Explainability is mandatory**
   The system must explain both action and inaction.

4. **Events matter more than single tweets**
   Repeated aligned posts should be merged into one narrative/event object.

5. **Deterministic decision layers**
   LLMs interpret; rule/policy layers decide; risk layers veto.

6. **Future-proofing**
   Architecture must leave room for simulations, decision logs, and future execution support.

---

## 7. Agentic AI Structure

The prior concept improves if we treat “agentic AI” as **bounded specialist modules**, not a free-form planner.

## 7.1 Recommended AI/Agent Modules

### A. Claim Extraction Agent

Input:

* raw X post
* quoted/replied-to context if relevant

Output:

* claim type
* direction
* time horizon
* certainty level
* mentioned entities/themes
* whether the post is actionable or just commentary

### B. Asset Mapping Agent

Input:

* extracted claim
* approved tradable universe

Output:

* mapped asset(s)
* mapping confidence
* rejected mappings

Constraint:

* may only map into the approved universe

### C. Event Clustering Agent

Input:

* multiple posts across sources and time

Output:

* grouped event/narrative cluster
* cluster summary
* source agreement/disagreement
* novelty and repetition signals

This becomes important because you explicitly want repeated aligned posts merged.

### D. Explanation Agent

Input:

* final decision object
* intermediate reasons and vetoes

Output:

* human-readable explanation:

  * why `BUY`, `HOLD`, or `SELL`
  * why not something else
  * what evidence mattered
  * what uncertainty remains

---

## 7.2 Non-agentic Core Layers

These should remain deterministic:

* source registry
* asset universe configuration
* policy engine
* market-context enrichment
* veto/risk engine
* audit log
* future simulation and locked-price page

This split is critical.
Otherwise the system becomes clever-looking but unreliable.

---

## 8. Functional Requirements

## 8.1 Source Monitoring

The system shall ingest posts from a curated list of X accounts within the accepted latency window.

## 8.2 Source Registry

The system shall maintain metadata for each source, including:

* source category
* baseline credibility
* preferred horizon
* policy template
* relevant sectors/assets
* special handling rules

Examples of categories:

* prediction-market/news
* macro commentator
* trader/opinion account
* official institution
* company/operator account

You already asked that different account types receive different treatment. This registry is how that happens.

## 8.3 Claim Extraction

The system shall transform raw posts into structured claims with:

* sentiment/direction
* claim type
* explicitness
* themes/entities
* horizon
* confidence

## 8.4 Asset Mapping

The system shall map claims only to the approved tradable universe.

## 8.5 Event Clustering

The system shall merge repeated aligned posts across monitored accounts into a single event object when appropriate.

Examples:

* multiple accounts pushing the same AI infrastructure thesis over 24–72 hours
* repeated references to a specific company, theme, or macro driver

## 8.6 Market Context Enrichment

The system shall enrich signal candidates with external market context.

Because you asked “any ideas?” on market context, the best v1 categories are:

### Recommended confirmation/context sources

* recent price trend over multi-day windows
* volatility regime
* volume anomalies for equities/ETFs
* sector ETF behavior
* broad market regime
* crypto market trend context, if crypto is in scope
* major news corroboration when relevant

This is enough for v1.
You do **not** need a giant data platform.

## 8.7 Policy Evaluation

The system shall evaluate whether a clustered event is eligible to influence a tradable asset using deterministic policy rules.

## 8.8 Decision Output

The system shall output:

* asset
* action (`BUY`, `HOLD`, `SELL`)
* confidence
* time horizon
* reasons
* blocked/vetoed status
* explanation text

## 8.9 Explainability

The system shall explain:

* why action was proposed
* why no action was proposed
* why a veto happened
* what accounts contributed
* what market context mattered
* what uncertainty remained

This is a major block, so it must be first-class, not an afterthought.

## 8.10 Placeholder Simulation / Decision Log Page

The system shall include a placeholder page for future:

* simulation runs
* decision logging
* price locking / reference price capture
* later replay and evaluation

This page does not need to simulate in v1, but the product must reserve the object model and UI entry point now.

---

## 9. Decision Framework

The system should not do:

`tweet -> LLM -> trade`

It should do:

`posts -> claims -> clustered event -> asset mapping -> market context -> policy evaluation -> veto/risk check -> decision`

## 9.1 Hard Checks First

Before any action is scored:

* is the source allowed?
* is the content actionable?
* does it map to an allowed asset?
* is the event new enough?
* is the mapping confidence acceptable?
* is this repetitive/stale?
* does market context support or contradict it?

## 9.2 Then Action Selection

Once the event survives hard checks, choose among:

* `BUY`
* `HOLD`
* `SELL`

## 9.3 Why no sizing yet

You mentioned “small/bigger sums,” but that should not enter v1 decision language yet.
It adds ambiguity fast.

A better approach:

* v1 returns directional recommendation only
* later versions can add conviction tiers or size bands

---

## 10. Market Context Proposal

You asked for ideas. Here is the practical v1 recommendation.

## 10.1 Equities / ETFs

Use:

* 5d / 10d / 20d returns
* relative performance vs sector ETF
* basic volume abnormality
* major market trend filter

## 10.2 Crypto

Use:

* 3d / 7d / 14d returns
* volatility
* broad crypto market direction
* optionally funding/open interest later, not mandatory in v1

## 10.3 Narrative Confirmation

For high-impact cases:

* one additional confirmation source such as major financial/news coverage or repeated signal convergence from monitored accounts

This is enough.
Do not overbuild this part initially.

---

## 11. Explainability Requirements

Each decision page/card should answer:

### If `BUY`

* what happened
* which accounts contributed
* why this maps to this asset
* what market context supports it
* why horizon is multi-day
* key risks / uncertainty

### If `SELL`

* same logic, but focused on deterioration, negative narrative, or adverse context

### If `HOLD`

* why the signal was insufficient or already priced
* what blocked escalation to buy/sell

### If vetoed

* exact veto reason:

  * weak mapping
  * stale signal
  * contradictory market context
  * low source confidence
  * excessive ambiguity
  * duplicate/redundant event

## 12. System Architecture

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Curated X Accounts] --> B[Ingestion Worker]
    B --> C[Raw Post Store]

    C --> D[Claim Extraction Agent]
    D --> E[Asset Mapping Agent]
    E --> F[Event Clustering Agent]

    F --> G[Structured Claims and Event Candidates]

    H[Source Registry] --> I[Policy Engine]
    J[Market Context Service] --> I
    G --> I

    I --> K[Risk and Veto Engine]

    K --> L[Decision Object]
    L --> M[Explainability Layer]
    M --> N[Dashboard and API]

    L --> O[Decision Log Store]
    O --> P[Placeholder Simulation and Locked-Price Page]

    K --> Q{Execution Enabled?}
    Q -->|No in v1| N
    Q -->|Future| R[Execution Layer]
    R --> S[Outcomes and Attribution]
    O --> S
```

## 13. Core Components

## 13.1 Ingestion Worker

Responsibilities:

* fetch monitored posts
* deduplicate
* persist raw content
* preserve timestamps and thread/quote relationships

## 13.2 Claim Extraction Agent

Responsibilities:

* convert noisy social text into structured claims
* classify whether post is news, opinion, rumor, commentary, or direct signal

## 13.3 Asset Mapping Agent

Responsibilities:

* connect claims to allowed assets only
* reject low-confidence mappings

## 13.4 Event Clustering Agent

Responsibilities:

* merge repeated aligned posts
* generate cluster-level summaries
* reduce noise from isolated tweets

## 13.5 Source Registry

Responsibilities:

* define how each account should be interpreted
* apply separate templates for:

  * Polymarket-like sources
  * trader/opinion accounts
  * future official sources

## 13.6 Market Context Service

Responsibilities:

* enrich events with price and market regime information
* help separate interesting from tradable

## 13.7 Policy Engine

Responsibilities:

* determine whether an event is eligible to affect an asset
* choose candidate action

## 13.8 Risk and Veto Engine

Responsibilities:

* block weak, stale, ambiguous, or contradicted signals
* downgrade overconfident but unsupported signals

## 13.9 Explainability Layer

Responsibilities:

* produce user-facing rationale
* expose reasons for action, hold, or veto

## 13.10 Placeholder Simulation / Locked-Price Page

Responsibilities:

* reserve future UI/backend entry point for:

  * price locking
  * replay
  * simulations
  * decision outcome tracking
